# MUTUAL MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING (MOU)

**THIS MUTUAL MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING** (hereinafter referred to as "the MOU") is made and entered into this **16th day of April 2026**.

### BETWEEN:

1.  **LIPAGAS LIMITED**, a limited liability company incorporated under the laws of Kenya, with its principal office at **Pine Tree Plaza, Kamburu Drive, Kilimani, Nairobi** (hereinafter referred to as "**LIPAGAS**" or the "**technology solutions partner**"), and of P.O. Box 233 - 00100, Nairobi, Kenya (Website: [lipagas.co](https://lipagas.co));

### AND:

2.  **STABEX INTERNATIONAL LIMITED**, a limited liability company incorporated under the Laws of the Republic of Kenya, with its registered office situated at Middle East Bank Towers, 5th Floor, Milimani Road, Nairobi, and of P.O. (LIPAGAS and STABEX are hereinafter collectively referred to as the "**Parties**" and individually as a "**Party**".)

---

### 1. RECITALS

**WHEREAS:**

A.  **STABEX** is a leading independent oil and gas marketing company in East Africa, involved in the supply, distribution, and retail of petroleum products, including Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG).

B.  **LIPAGAS** is a clean energy technology company that provides innovative solutions for LPG distribution, including a proprietary digital platform for last-mile delivery, Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) metering technologies, and sales transformation tools.

C.  The Parties are desirous of establishing a strategic partnership based on mutual trust and growth, leveraging LIPAGAS’s technology and STABEX’s market presence to enhance the distribution of LPG, increase offtake, and grow the overall market share of STABEX products in Kenya.

D.  **The Spirit of Partnership**: This MOU is entered into in a spirit of mutual support and professional safety, where both Parties aim to secure their commercial interests while fostering an environment of innovation, shared prosperity, and long-term strategic alignment.

**NOW THEREFORE IT IS AGREED AS FOLLOWS:**

---

### 2. OBJECTIVES AND SCOPE OF COLLABORATION

The primary objective of this MOU is to establish a framework for cooperation between the Parties in the following areas:

**2.1. Last-Mile Delivery & Distribution**
LIPAGAS shall provide its digital application and logistics infrastructure to facilitate last-mile delivery of STABEX LPG products to end-consumers, wholesalers, and vendors.

**2.2. Technology Integration**
STABEX shall integrate the LIPAGAS Application into its distribution network to streamline ordering, fulfillment, and real-time tracking of LPG deliveries.

**2.3. Bulk LPG Collaboration**
The Parties shall collaborate on the procurement, logistics, and supply of bulk LPG to optimize cost efficiencies and ensure consistent availability for both industrial and domestic segments.

**2.4. Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) Solutions**
LIPAGAS shall implement its proprietary PAYG technology for:
- **LPG Reticulation**: Smart metering solutions for apartments and multi-unit residential complexes.
- **Retail PAYG**: Smart devices for small-capacity cylinders to allow low-income users to pay for gas as they consume.

**2.5. Sales Transformation and Market Growth**
LIPAGAS shall deploy its proprietary technologies designed to transform sales processes, increase customer retention, and drive offtake of STABEX petroleum products, aiming for a significant increase in STABEX's market share.

---

### 3. MUTUAL SYNERGY AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**3.1. Collaborative Responsibilities of LIPAGAS:**
- Deploy and support the LIPAGAS App to empower STABEX vendors and customers.
- Provide reliable technology for PAYG hardware and software, ensuring steady performance for reticulation and retail segments.
- Share data-driven insights and proprietary sales tools to assist STABEX in optimizing offtake.
- Provide active support in logistical coordination for efficient last-mile delivery.

**3.2. Collaborative Responsibilities of STABEX:**
- Provide a steady and reliable supply of LPG and petroleum products to meet the technology-driven demand.
- Facilitate the onboarding of its wholesalers, vendors, and distributors to the shared digital ecosystem.
- Lead marketing and joint-brand awareness to promote the technology-enhanced services.
- Ensure all operations meet and maintain the highest safety and regulatory standards.

---

### 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

4.1. All proprietary technologies, software code, patents, trademarks, trade secrets, and technical know-how owned by LIPAGAS (including but not limited to the LIPAGAS App, smart metering modules, PAYG hardware, algorithms, and data structures) shall remain the exclusive property of LIPAGAS.

4.2. STABEX is granted a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, and revocable license to use the LIPAGAS technologies solely for the authorized purposes defined in this MOU.

4.3. **Strict Prohibitions**: STABEX shall not, and shall not permit any third party to:
- (a) Reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code or hardware architecture of LIPAGAS technologies.
- (b) Modify, adapt, or create derivative works based on LIPAGAS technologies.
- (c) Rent, lease, sublicense, or redistribute LIPAGAS software or hardware to any third party without express written consent.
- (d) Remove or obscure any copyright, trademark, or proprietary rights notices.

---

### 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1. The Parties agree to keep confidential all non-public information obtained from the other Party, including but not limited to business plans, customer data, pricing models, and technical specifications.

5.2. This obligation shall survive the termination of this MOU for a period of two (2) years.

---

### 6. DATA PROTECTION & PRIVACY

6.1. The Parties shall comply with the **Data Protection Act, 2019 (Laws of Kenya)** and its accompanying regulations in the collection, processing, and storage of any personal data arising from the performance of this MOU.

6.2. **Compliance & Registration**: The Parties acknowledge their obligations under the Data Protection Act and commit to obtaining and maintaining all necessary registrations with the **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)** as required by law. Both Parties shall treat data in accordance with the Act's principles pending the finalization of any administrative registrations.

6.3. **Roles**: For the purposes of this collaboration, **LIPAGAS** shall be considered the **Data Controller** of the technical platform and customer application data. **STABEX** shall act as a **Joint Controller or Data Processor** depending on the specific operational touchpoint within the distribution chain.

6.4. **Data Sharing Agreement (DSA)**: Prior to the exchange of any personal customer data, the Parties agree to execute a formal **Data Sharing Agreement (DSA)** in accordance with the ODPC guidelines, detailing technical security measures, breach notification protocols, and data minimization principles.

---

### 7. COMMERCIAL TERMS AND FINANCIAL ARRANGEMENTS

**7.1. Delivery Fees**
LIPAGAS shall be entitled to charge a delivery fee for the transportation and distribution of products via its platform. The delivery fee shall be set based on prevailing market rates in the relevant geographical regions.

**7.2. PAYG Commissions**
LIPAGAS shall be entitled to a commission on all revenue generated through the Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) metering and reticulation systems.

**7.3. Mutual Agreement and Revenue Share**
The specific fee structures, commission percentages, and/or revenue-sharing ratios shall be mutually agreed upon by the Parties in writing and shall be documented in a definitive Service Level Agreement (SLA) or a Commercial Appendix to this MOU.

---

### 8. LEGAL EFFECT

8.1. This MOU is a statement of intent and does not create legally binding obligations on either Party, save for clauses 4 (Intellectual Property), 5 (Confidentiality), 7.3 (Mutual Agreement), and 11 (Governing Law and Dispute Resolution).

8.2. The Parties intend to execute definitive Service Level Agreements (SLAs) or Commercial Contracts to govern specific operational terms.

9.1. This MOU shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in force for an initial period of [Insert, e.g., 2 years], unless terminated earlier.

### 10. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

10.1. STABEX agrees not to bypass, circumvent, or avoid LIPAGAS to utilize the same or substantially similar proprietary technologies provided by LIPAGAS through any third party during the term of this MOU.

---

### 11. FORCE MAJEURE

11.1. Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this MOU if such failure or delay is caused by an event of Force Majeure.

11.2. **Definition**: Force Majeure refers to any event beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party, including but not limited to:
- Acts of God (fire, flood, earthquake, or other natural disasters);
- War, riots, or civil unrest;
- **Telecommunication and Internet Outages**: Widespread failures of the internet, cellular networks, or third-party digital infrastructure essential for the operation of the LIPAGAS platform;
- **Regulatory Actions**: Government-mandated shutdowns, changes in law, or executive orders that directly impact the energy or digital technology sectors.

11.3. The Party affected by Force Majeure shall immediately notify the other Party and shall be excused from performance for the duration of the event.

---

### 12. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1. This MOU shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the **Laws of the Republic of Kenya**.

12.2. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this MOU shall be resolved amicably through good-faith negotiations. If not resolved within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration in according with the **Arbitration Act, 1995 (Laws of Kenya)**.

---

### 13. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

13.1. **Severability**: If any provision of this MOU is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

13.2. **Entire Agreement**: This MOU constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding the subject matter herein and supersedes all prior agreements or understandings, whether written or oral.

13.3. **No Waiver**: The failure of either Party to enforce any right or provision of this MOU shall not constitute a waiver of such right or provision.

13.4. **Counterparts**: This MOU may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
