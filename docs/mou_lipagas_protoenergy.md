### 1. RECITALS

**WHEREAS:**

A.  **PROTO ENERGY** is a leading energy company in Kenya engaged in the business of supply, distribution, and marketing of Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) products.

B.  **LIPAGAS** is an innovative technology solutions partner providing proprietary omni-channel digital platforms and technologies (accessible via WhatsApp chatbots, native mobile applications, web portals, and APIs) designed to optimize LPG last-mile delivery, empower distributors, and drive sales transformation.

C.  **PROTO ENERGY** desires to utilize the LIPAGAS digital application and ecosystem for its last-mile delivery operations, encompassing both direct-to-consumer fulfillment and distribution to wholesalers and vendors.

D.  The Parties are desirous of establishing a mutual, strategic partnership. Through the deployment of LIPAGAS's proprietary technologies, the collaboration aims to transform sales processes, increase the offtake of LPG and related products/services, and ultimately drive significant growth in the market share of PROTO ENERGY.

E.  **The Spirit of Partnership**: This MOU is entered into in a spirit of mutual support and professional safety, where both Parties aim to secure their commercial interests while fostering an environment of innovation, shared prosperity, and long-term strategic alignment.

**NOW THEREFORE IT IS AGREED AS FOLLOWS:**

---

### 2. OBJECTIVES AND SCOPE OF COLLABORATION

The primary objective of this MOU is to establish a framework for cooperation between the Parties for the following purposes:

**2.1. Integration of the LipaGas Digital Ecosystem**
PROTO ENERGY shall utilize the proprietary LIPAGAS digital ecosystem—which is fully accessible and integrated across WhatsApp, native mobile applications, web portals, and APIs—to formalize and efficiently manage its last-mile delivery and distribution routes, targeting end-consumers, bulk wholesalers, and retail vendors.

**2.2. Last-Mile Delivery Support**
LIPAGAS shall provide operational and technological support in the form of delivery and logistics facilitation to complement PROTO ENERGY’s existing supply chain. This includes the real-time tracking, dispatching, and management of LPG deliveries.

**2.3. Sales Transformation, AI, and Trade Optimization**
LIPAGAS shall deploy its proprietary technologies and data analytics modules—including embedded Artificial Intelligence (AI) for trade optimization, predictive sales scaling, and operational efficiency—designed strictly to transform the traditional LPG sales model. By leveraging these AI-driven tools, PROTO ENERGY shall be capable of increasing the offtake of its LPG products and services, maximizing route efficiency, fostering customer loyalty, and expanding its overall market share.

**2.4. Mutual Technology & Logistics Strategy**
Both Parties shall collaborate on strategic initiatives leveraging LIPAGAS's digital infrastructure to unlock new markets, streamline wholesaler ordering, and empower vendors within the PROTO ENERGY retail network.

**2.5. Pay-As-You-Go (PAYG) Solutions**
LIPAGAS shall implement its proprietary PAYG technology for the benefit of PROTO ENERGY, specifically targeting:
- **LPG Reticulation**: Smart metering solutions for apartments, multi-unit residential complexes, and commercial setups.
- **Retail PAYG**: Smart devices for small-capacity cylinders to allow low-income retail users to pay for gas precisely as they consume it.

---

### 3. MUTUAL SYNERGY AND RESPONSIBILITIES

**3.1. Collaborative Responsibilities of LIPAGAS:**
- Deploy, maintain, and offer robust technical support for the LIPAGAS App tailored to PROTO ENERGY’s distribution network.
- Provide proprietary sales transformation tools, insights, and data analytics to actively assist PROTO ENERGY in expanding market reach and LPG offtake.
- Assist, where agreed, in logistical coordination and operational execution of last-mile LPG deliveries.
- Ensure the application remains agile, secure, and fully operational for vendors, wholesalers, and end-consumers.

**3.2. Collaborative Responsibilities of PROTO ENERGY:**
- Adopt and integrate the LIPAGAS App as a primary digital channel for order fulfillment and last-mile logistics routing.
- Provide a steady, reliable supply of LPG and petroleum products corresponding to the technological boost in demand.
- Support the onboarding and training of its wholesalers, distributors, and delivery personnel onto the LIPAGAS digital ecosystem.
- Lead joint marketing initiatives to promote these technology-enhanced services to the public.

### 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS

4.1. All proprietary technologies, software code, patents, trademarks, trade secrets, algorithms, and technical know-how owned by LIPAGAS (including but not limited to the LIPAGAS App and its underlying data architectures) shall remain the exclusive digital property of LIPAGAS.

4.2. PROTO ENERGY is granted a limited, non-exclusive, non-transferable, and revocable license to utilize the LIPAGAS technologies solely for the authorized purposes defined in this MOU.

4.3. **Strict Prohibitions**: PROTO ENERGY shall not, and shall not permit any third party to:
- (a) Reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code or underlying architecture of LIPAGAS technologies.
- (b) Modify, adapt, or create derivative works based on LIPAGAS platforms.
- (c) Rent, lease, sublicense, or redistribute LIPAGAS software to any third party without express written consent.
- (d) Remove or obscure any copyright, trademark, or proprietary rights notices on the LIPAGAS application.

4.4. **Data Ownership and Analytics**: LIPAGAS shall retain full, exclusive ownership of all aggregated, anonymized platform usage data, transactional trends, and analytical insights generated through the use of its technology suite. LIPAGAS reserves the right to unrestrictedly use such anonymized data for platform improvement, analytics, and broader commercial purposes.

4.5. **API and Systems Integrity**: PROTO ENERGY is expressly prohibited from connecting, integrating, or linking any unauthorized third-party applications, plugins, or software to the LIPAGAS APIs or core infrastructure without the prior written technical approval of LIPAGAS.

### 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1. The Parties agree to keep confidential all non-public information obtained from the other Party, including but not limited to business plans, customer data, vendor databases, pricing models, and technical specifications.

5.2. This obligation shall survive the termination of this MOU for a period of two (2) years.

---

### 6. DATA PROTECTION & PRIVACY

6.1. The Parties shall comply with the **Data Protection Act, 2019 (Laws of Kenya)** and its accompanying regulations in the collection, processing, and storage of any personal data generated from the performance of this MOU.

6.2. **Compliance**: Both Parties commit to maintaining all necessary registrations with the **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)** as required by Kenyan law.

6.3. **Roles**: For this partnership, **LIPAGAS** shall act as a Data Controller of the underlying technical platform and app-user metadata, while **PROTO ENERGY** shall act as a Joint Controller or Data Processor concerning their direct vendor and customer transactional data.

6.4. The Parties shall execute a distinct **Data Sharing Agreement (DSA)** prior to the structured exchange of any sensitive customer data.

---

### 7. COMMERCIAL TERMS AND FINANCIAL ARRANGEMENTS

**7.1. Technology & Delivery Fees**
Subject to mutual agreement by both Parties, LIPAGAS may charge a software licensing or transaction-based fee for utilizing its proprietary application. Where LIPAGAS provides direct logistical support and last-mile fulfillment, LIPAGAS shall be entitled to charge fees based on prevailing market rates. For indicative purposes and without strictly restricting future pricing adjustments, current last-mile delivery fees typically range between KES 100 to KES 350 per delivery, while vendor fulfillment fees are generally between KES 3 to KES 10 per Kilogram (Kg).

**7.2. Mutual Agreement on Fee Structures**
The specific commercial terms, platform fees, revenue-sharing models, and delivery charges shall be mutually agreed upon in writing and documented in a definitive Service Level Agreement (SLA) or Commercial Appendix.

8.1. This MOU is a statement of intent and does not create legally binding obligations on either Party, save for clauses 4 (Intellectual Property), 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Data Protection & Privacy), 11 (Liability and Indemnification), 12 (Non-Solicitation), 14 (Publicity and Marketing), 15 (Post-Termination Obligations), 16.5 (Assignment), 16.6 (Survival), and 13 (Governing Law and Dispute Resolution).

8.2. The Parties intend to execute definitive Service Level Agreements (SLAs) or Commercial Contracts to govern detailed operational commitments.

### 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1. This MOU shall commence on the Effective Date and remain in force for an initial period of **one (1) year**, unless terminated earlier.

9.2. Either Party may terminate this MOU by giving thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party.

9.3. **Termination for Material Breach**: Notwithstanding clause 9.2, LIPAGAS reserves the right to terminate this MOU and suspend all access to its digital ecosystem immediately and without prior notice in the event of a material breach by PROTO ENERGY of clauses 4 (Intellectual Property), 5 (Confidentiality), or 10 (Non-Circumvention).

---

### 10. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

10.1. PROTO ENERGY agrees not to bypass, circumvent, or avoid LIPAGAS by commissioning, developing, or adopting any third-party digital platform that incorporates, derives from, or utilizes any intellectual property, API structures, or proprietary methodologies shared by LIPAGAS under this MOU.

### 11. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

11.1. **"As-Is" Provision and Limitation of Liability**: The LIPAGAS technologies and platforms are provided on an "as-is" basis during the currency of this MOU. LIPAGAS provides no strict uptime or uninterrupted service guarantees unless explicitly defined in a subsequent Service Level Agreement (SLA). To the maximum extent permitted by law, LIPAGAS shall not be liable to PROTO ENERGY for any indirect, consequential, or special damages, including lost revenue or profits, arising out of any platform downtime or technical interruptions.

11.2. **Indemnification for LPG Handling**: PROTO ENERGY assumes full legal and operational responsibility for the physical safety, quality, regulatory compliance, and handling of LPG products. PROTO ENERGY agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless LIPAGAS, its directors, and employees against any and all claims, damages, liabilities, or lawsuits arising from defective cylinders, gas-related accidents, or violations of energy and safety regulations (e.g., EPRA regulations).

### 12. NON-SOLICITATION

12.1. During the term of this MOU and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination or expiration, PROTO ENERGY agrees that it shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, hire, recruit, or attempt to solicit or hire any employee, developer, or primary contractor of LIPAGAS without the express written consent of LIPAGAS.

---

### 13. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

13.1. This MOU shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the **Laws of the Republic of Kenya**.

13.2. **Escalation Matrix**: The Parties agree to act in good faith to resolve any disputes arising out of or in connection with this MOU through a formal step-by-step escalation process prior to commencing arbitration.
- **Step 1:** Initial operational disputes shall be referred to the respective Project Managers.
- **Step 2:** If unresolved within seven (7) days, the dispute shall be escalated to the Managing Directors or C-Level executives of both Parties.
- **Step 3:** Only if the Managing Directors fail to resolve the dispute amicably within fourteen (14) days from the formal escalation date, either Party may proceed to arbitration.

13.3. **Arbitration**: If not resolved through the Escalation Matrix outlined in clause 13.2, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration in accordance with the **Arbitration Act, 1995 (Laws of Kenya)**.

---

### 14. PUBLICITY AND MARKETING

14.1. **Brand Recognition**: Each Party grants to the other a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use the other Party’s name and logo in its marketing materials, website, and investor presentations to identify the other Party as a strategic partner.

14.2. **"Powered by LipaGas"**: PROTO ENERGY agrees to allow the phrase "Powered by LipaGas" or similar attribution to be displayed within the digital interfaces (WhatsApp, App, etc.) used by end-consumers and vendors, subject to brand guideline alignment.

14.3. **Press Releases**: Any formal press release or public announcement regarding this partnership shall be subject to mutual written approval by both Parties.

---

### 15. POST-TERMINATION OBLIGATIONS

15.1. **Hardware Recovery**: Upon expiration or termination of this MOU for any reason, PROTO ENERGY shall provide all necessary assistance and access to facilitate the prompt recovery of any LIPAGAS-owned hardware, smart meters, or PAYG modules within thirty (30) days.

15.2. **Data Export**: Upon request within thirty (30) days of termination, LIPAGAS shall provide PROTO ENERGY with a final export of its transactional and vendor data in a standard digital format. LIPAGAS reserves the right to charge a reasonable administrative fee for such data processing and handover services.

---

### 16. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

16.1. **Severability**: If any provision of this MOU is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

16.2. **Entire Agreement**: This MOU constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties regarding the subject matter herein and supersedes all prior agreements or understandings, whether written or oral.

16.3. **Force Majeure**: Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this MOU if such failure or delay is caused by an event of Force Majeure (including acts of God, natural disasters, telecommunications outages, or government actions).

16.4. **Counterparts**: This MOU may be executed in any number of counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, but all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.

16.5. **Assignment**: Neither Party may assign or transfer its rights or obligations under this MOU to any third party without the prior written consent of the other Party; provided, however, that LIPAGAS may assign this MOU without consent to a successor in interest in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

16.6. **Survival**: The provisions of clauses 4 (Intellectual Property), 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Data Protection), 8.1 (Legal Effect), 11 (Liability), 12 (Non-Solicitation), 13 (Governing Law), 15 (Post-Termination), and this clause 16.6 shall survive the expiration or termination of this MOU.
